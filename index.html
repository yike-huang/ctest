A quieter way to meet what you are carrying today.

Inner Canvas offers short, art-inspired coping activities for people living with and beyond cancer. It helps you notice emotions, choose a gentle creative exercise, and find reliable support resources.

This website supports reflection and coping. It does not provide medical diagnosis, psychotherapy, emergency care, or treatment advice.

Today does not need to be solved. It can be witnessed.

      Support and safety

      You do not need to handle this moment alone.

      This website cannot provide emergency care. If you may hurt yourself, feel unsafe, or need immediate help, contact emergency services now or reach out to a crisis line in your area.

      Call 988 in the United States Visit 988 Lifeline Find cancer support services

      Contact your care team

      Oncology nurses, social workers, palliative care teams, and mental health professionals can help with distress.

      Tell one trusted person

      A simple message can be enough: "I am having a hard moment and I do not want to be alone with it."

      Use the app gently

      If a creative activity feels too heavy, stop. Seeking human support can be the next right step.

      Inner Canvas is a first-version prototype for supportive reflection, not medical or mental health care.
